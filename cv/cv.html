Summary

As an undergraduate at UC Irvine, I double-majored in biology and chemistry and researched the interactions between the human immune system and the parasite Toxoplasma gondii. In graduate school, I have switched to studying cancer biology using computational methods. While learning about statistics and mathematical modeling, I was naturally drawn to Bayesian statistical modeling which has since become a passion of mine.
